About My Education

After moving to Canada, I had all of my education here. I never went to school in China before. I went to elementary school at Trafalgar in Vancouver and in grade 3, I transferred to West Point Grey Academy. Even though I grew up in Canada, English was always hard for me, so transferring to a private school made it harder.

I went to WPGA from grade 3 to when I graduated from high school. During that time, I learnt a lot of chinese outside of school. In high school, I started having interest in Korean culture, so I started to study korean on my own time. After graduating from high school, I went to University of Toronto and studied computer science for two years. I found it very challenging and was considering switching programs. This was when I decided to come to BCIT because Vancouver was my hometown and I found that in big universities such as U of T, they teach many things very theory based. It wasn't super practical.
